About Indian Art:

Indian art consists of a variety of art forms, including painting, sculpture, pottery, and textile arts such as woven silk. Geographically, it spans the entire Indian subcontinent, including India, Pakistan, Bangladesh, Sri Lanka, Nepal, Bhutan, and eastern Afghanistan.

A strong sense of design is characteristic of Indian art and can be observed in both modern and traditional forms. Influenced by religions like Hinduism, Buddhism, Jainism, Sikhism, and Islam, Indian art reflects spiritual depth and cultural richness.

From the prehistoric cave paintings of Bhimbetka to the majestic sculptures of Khajuraho and the contemporary strokes of M.F. Hussain, every piece tells a story of tradition, devotion, and creativity.
